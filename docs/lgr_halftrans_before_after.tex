\section*{Notation}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{cl}
\toprule
Symbol & Meaning \\
\midrule
$\mathbf{x}_C$      & centroid of the coarse cell $C$ \\
$\mathbf{x}_L$      & centroid of the fine (LGR) cell $L$ \\
$\mathbf{x}_{F_C}$  & centre of the parent face $F_C$
                       (full face of $C$ before LGR subdivision) \\
$\mathbf{x}_{F_L}$  & centre of the sub-face $F_L$
                       (the actual face shared by $C$ and $L$) \\
$\hat{\mathbf{n}}$  & unit outward normal to the interface \\
$d_\perp$           & perpendicular distance from $\mathbf{x}_C$
                       to the face plane:
                       $d_\perp = (\mathbf{x}_{F_L}-\mathbf{x}_C)
                       \cdot\hat{\mathbf{n}}$ \\
$A_L$               & area of sub-face $F_L$ \\
$k_C,\;k_L$         & permeability of $C$ and $L$ normal to the face \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

% ------------------------------------------------------------------
\section*{The TPFA formula}
% ------------------------------------------------------------------

For any cell--face pair the half-transmissibility is
\begin{equation}
  T_\mathrm{half} \;=\; k\;\frac{A\;|\mathbf{d}\cdot\hat{\mathbf{n}}|}
                                 {|\mathbf{d}|^{2}},
\end{equation}
where $\mathbf{d}$ is the vector from the cell centroid to the face
point used in the computation.  Writing $\mathbf{d} =
d_\perp\,\hat{\mathbf{n}} + \mathbf{d}_\parallel$ gives
\begin{equation}
  T_\mathrm{half} \;=\; k\;\frac{A\;d_\perp}
                                 {d_\perp^2 + |\mathbf{d}_\parallel|^2}.
\end{equation}
When $\mathbf{d}_\parallel = \mathbf{0}$ this reduces to the
K-orthogonal formula $k A / d_\perp$.

The interface transmissibility is the harmonic mean:
\begin{equation}
  T_{CL} \;=\;
  \left(\frac{1}{T_{\mathrm{half},C}}+\frac{1}{T_{\mathrm{half},L}}\right)^{-1}.
\end{equation}

% ------------------------------------------------------------------
\section*{Before}
% ------------------------------------------------------------------

The coarse cell used the \textbf{parent face centre} $\mathbf{x}_{F_C}$;
the fine cell used its own sub-face centre $\mathbf{x}_{F_L}$.

\begin{align}
  \mathbf{d}_C^{\,\text{before}} &= \mathbf{x}_{F_C} - \mathbf{x}_C, \\[6pt]
  \mathbf{d}_L                   &= \mathbf{x}_{F_L} - \mathbf{x}_L.
\end{align}

\begin{align}
  T_{\mathrm{half},C}^{\,\text{before}}
    &= k_C\;\frac{A_L\;\bigl|(\mathbf{x}_{F_C}-\mathbf{x}_C)
                              \cdot\hat{\mathbf{n}}\bigr|}
                 {|\mathbf{x}_{F_C}-\mathbf{x}_C|^{2}},
    \label{eq:Tbefore_C}\\[10pt]
  T_{\mathrm{half},L}^{\,\text{before}}
    &= k_L\;\frac{A_L\;\bigl|(\mathbf{x}_{F_L}-\mathbf{x}_L)
                              \cdot\hat{\mathbf{n}}\bigr|}
                 {|\mathbf{x}_{F_L}-\mathbf{x}_L|^{2}}.
    \label{eq:Tbefore_L}
\end{align}

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Behaviour.}
On a Cartesian grid $\mathbf{x}_{F_C}$ lies directly in front of
$\mathbf{x}_C$, so $\mathbf{d}_\parallel = \mathbf{0}$ and
$T_{\mathrm{half},C}^{\,\text{before}} = k_C A_L / d_\perp$.
On a non-Cartesian grid $\mathbf{x}_{F_C}$ is laterally displaced from
$\mathbf{x}_C$, introducing a tangential component and a small error.
Because that displacement differs from one coarse cell to another, the
result was \textbf{not symmetric} across the LGR boundary on irregular
grids.

% ------------------------------------------------------------------
\section*{After}
% ------------------------------------------------------------------

The coarse cell now uses only the \textbf{perpendicular component} of
the distance to the face plane; the fine cell is unchanged.

\begin{align}
  d_\perp
    &= (\mathbf{x}_{F_L}-\mathbf{x}_C)\cdot\hat{\mathbf{n}}, \\[6pt]
  \mathbf{d}_C^{\,\text{after}}
    &= d_\perp\,\hat{\mathbf{n}}, \label{eq:dafter_C}\\[6pt]
  \mathbf{d}_L
    &= \mathbf{x}_{F_L} - \mathbf{x}_L.
\end{align}

\begin{align}
  T_{\mathrm{half},C}^{\,\text{after}}
    &= k_C\;\frac{A_L\;d_\perp}{d_\perp^{2}}
     = \frac{k_C\,A_L}{d_\perp}
     = \frac{k_C\,A_L}
            {(\mathbf{x}_{F_L}-\mathbf{x}_C)\cdot\hat{\mathbf{n}}},
    \label{eq:Tafter_C}\\[10pt]
  T_{\mathrm{half},L}^{\,\text{after}}
    &= k_L\;\frac{A_L\;\bigl|(\mathbf{x}_{F_L}-\mathbf{x}_L)
                              \cdot\hat{\mathbf{n}}\bigr|}
                 {|\mathbf{x}_{F_L}-\mathbf{x}_L|^{2}}.
    \label{eq:Tafter_L}
\end{align}

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Behaviour.}
By construction $\mathbf{d}_\parallel = \mathbf{0}$, so the K-orthogonal
formula is recovered on every grid.
The value $d_\perp$ is the distance from $\mathbf{x}_C$ to the face
\emph{plane}: it is the same for every sub-face of the same parent face,
so $T_{\mathrm{half},C}^{\,\text{after}}$ is \textbf{identical for all
sub-faces} (symmetry guaranteed).
Note that $T_{\mathrm{half},L}$ is \textbf{unchanged}.

\section*{Comparison}
% ------------------------------------------------------------------

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lcc}
\toprule
 & \textbf{Before} & \textbf{After} \\
\midrule
Face point for $C$
  & $\mathbf{x}_{F_C}$ (parent face centre)
  & $d_\perp\,\hat{\mathbf{n}}$ (normal projection) \\[4pt]
Face point for $L$
  & $\mathbf{x}_{F_L}$
  & $\mathbf{x}_{F_L}$ \quad (unchanged) \\[4pt]
$\mathbf{d}_\parallel = \mathbf{0}$ for $C$
  & Cartesian grids only
  & Always \\[4pt]
$T_{\mathrm{half},C} = k_C A_L / d_\perp$
  & Cartesian grids only
  & Always \\[4pt]
Same $T_{\mathrm{half},C}$ for all sub-faces
  & Always
  & Always \\[4pt]
Symmetric on non-Cartesian grids
  & No
  & Yes \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

% ------------------------------------------------------------------
\section*{Numerical example: HORIZONTAL-WELL-REFINED}
% ------------------------------------------------------------------

\begin{center}
$\Delta x = 300\,\text{m}$, \quad
$\Delta z = 25\,\text{m}$, \quad
LGR with $2\times$ I-refinement.
\end{center}

At the K-direction coarse--fine interface:
\[
  d_\perp = \frac{\Delta z}{2} = 12.5\,\text{m}, \qquad
  |\mathbf{d}_\parallel^{\text{before}}|
    = 0\,\text{m} \quad \text{(Cartesian grid)}.
\]

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lccc}
\toprule
 & $|\mathbf{d}_C|^2\;[\mathrm{m}^2]$
 & $T_{\mathrm{half},C}$
 & Symmetric? \\
\midrule
Before & $12.5^2 = 156$ & $k_C A_L / 12.5$ & No (non-Cartesian) \\
After  & $12.5^2 = 156$ & $k_C A_L / 12.5$ & Yes \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\medskip\noindent
For this Cartesian test case the transmissibility value is the same
before and after; the gain is the restoration of symmetry on non-Cartesian
grids and the elimination of the asymmetry bug.
